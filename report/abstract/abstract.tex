\section*{Abstract}
\label{sec:abstract}

Automated chest X-ray (CXR) interpretation is vital for rapid clinical decision-making in emergency and inpatient settings. However, most automated baselines are image-centric, ignoring the clinical context that clinicians routinely use. This thesis studies whether clinical context and prior-study information improve long-tailed chest X-ray classification. We evaluate a prior-aware multimodal CXR classification model that integrates current radiographs, free-text clinical indications, vital signs, and prior-study context using modality-specific encoders and a transformer-based fusion module. Evaluated on the filtered CXR-LT-aligned test split, the proposed model achieves a mean Average Precision (mAP) of 0.5662 and a macro AUROC (mAUROC) of 0.9026, outperforming the CaMCheX (Image-only) baseline (0.3246 mAP) and a multimodal baseline without prior information (0.4098 mAP). Prior-study integration yields the largest improvement on rare tail classes, increasing mAP from 0.1586 to 0.4076. These results show that prior-study context and multimodal fusion improve diagnostic performance and tail-class sensitivity.
