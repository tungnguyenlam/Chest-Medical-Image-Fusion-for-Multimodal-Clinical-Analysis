\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}

This section reviews the existing literature and baseline approaches relevant to multimodal medical image classification, clinical text modeling, vital signs processing, and temporal patient history modeling.

\subsection{Single-Modal Chest X-Ray Classification}
\label{subsec:related_single_modal}

Review key architectures and benchmarks in automated chest radiograph analysis. Mention landmark datasets like ChestX-ray14, CheXpert, and MIMIC-CXR. Discuss standard CNN backbones (e.g., DenseNet, ResNet) and modern transformer-based/convolutional backbones (e.g., ViT, ConvNeXt-V2) used to classify pathologies from single or multiple image views (frontal and lateral).

\subsection{Multimodal Clinical Fusion}
\label{subsec:related_multimodal}

Discuss existing architectures that fuse chest X-rays with non-image clinical data:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Early vs. Late Fusion}: Compare architectures that concatenate features at early stages versus late classifier stages.
    \item \textbf{Text and Image Alignment}: Review models (e.g., ConVIRT, BioViL) that use contrastive learning to align radiographs with radiology reports.
    \item \textbf{EHR and Vitals Integration}: Discuss how structured data (demographics, vital signs, lab values) are typically fused. Critique the practice of translating numeric variables into text prompts for pre-trained language models.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Temporal and Prior-Aware Medical Modeling}
\label{subsec:related_temporal}

Review studies that incorporate historical patient records (longitudinal studies) to improve current diagnostic accuracy:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Discuss models that compare a current image with a prior image using Siamese networks, image difference maps, or cross-attention.
    \item Highlight the challenges of prior-aware modeling, such as variable time gaps, missing historical records, and the risk of "shortcut learning" where the model over-relies on prior labels or reports.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Mitigating Class Imbalance in Medical Datasets}
\label{subsec:related_imbalance}

Review loss functions and techniques designed to handle the highly skewed class distributions common in clinical datasets:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Compare standard Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) with Focal Loss.
    \item Discuss the formulation and benefits of Asymmetric Loss (ASL), which targets negative and positive samples with different focusing parameters to prevent easy negatives from overwhelming the gradient.
\end{itemize}
